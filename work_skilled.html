Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about skilled migration

General Questions

What is skilled migration and who qualifies? ‚ñº

Skilled migration allows qualified professionals to work and live permanently in another country based on their skills, qualifications, and work experience. To qualify, you typically need: (1) A qualification or trade skill in demand, (2) Relevant work experience (usually 2-5+ years), (3) English language proficiency, (4) To meet age requirements (varies by country), and (5) Good health and character. Each country has specific occupation lists and points systems.

Do I need a job offer to apply for skilled migration? ‚ñº

It depends on the country and pathway. Australia (189), Canada (Express Entry), and New Zealand (SMC) allow you to apply without a job offer through points-based systems. However, UK, Germany, and UAE require a job offer from a licensed sponsor. Having a job offer can boost your points even in countries where it's not mandatory.

How long does the entire migration process take? ‚ñº

The timeline varies significantly: Skill assessment (8-16 weeks), English test (4-8 weeks), EOI/profile creation (1-2 weeks), Waiting for invitation (1-12+ months depending on draw frequency and your points), Visa processing after invitation (3-12 months). Total average timeline: 12-24 months from initial assessment to visa grant. Express pathways exist for in-demand occupations.

Points & Eligibility

How many points do I need to migrate? ‚ñº

Minimum requirements vary: Australia (189) - 65 points minimum, but competitive scores are 85-95+; Canada Express Entry - No minimum, but competitive CRS is 470-490+; New Zealand - 160 points to submit EOI. Points are awarded for age, education, work experience, English proficiency, and other factors. We help optimize your profile to maximize points.

What if my occupation is not on the skilled occupation list? ‚ñº

If your occupation isn't listed, you have several options: (1) Look for closely related occupations that match your duties, (2) Consider employer-sponsored visas (which often have broader occupation lists), (3) Explore regional/provincial nomination programs with extended lists, (4) Check other countries where your occupation may be in demand, or (5) Consider upskilling or gaining qualifications in a listed occupation. We assess all viable alternatives during consultation.

Can I improve my points score? ‚ñº

Yes! Common strategies include: Improving English test scores (can add 10-20 points), Gaining additional work experience, Completing higher education (Masters/PhD), Getting partner assessed for skills, Securing state/provincial nomination (+5-15 points), Professional year programs in Australia, Learning French for Canada. We provide personalized strategies to boost your competitiveness.

Family & Dependents

Can I include my family in my application? ‚ñº

Yes! You can include your spouse/partner and dependent children (typically under 18, sometimes under 23 if studying) in your primary application. They receive the same visa rights as you. Additional costs apply for dependent assessments, medicals, and police checks. In points-based systems, a skilled partner can actually increase your points.
