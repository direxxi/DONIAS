Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about business visas

What's the difference between investor visa and entrepreneur visa? ‚ñº

Investor visas typically require passive investment (bonds, funds, property) without requiring active business management. Examples: Portugal Golden Visa, UAE Golden Visa. Entrepreneur/Innovator visas require you to actively run and build a business, often with job creation requirements and endorsements. Examples: UK Innovator Founder, Canada Start-Up Visa. Investor visas usually need higher capital but less involvement; entrepreneur visas need lower capital but more hands-on management.

Do I need to move to the country immediately? ‚ñº

Depends on the program. Minimal stay requirements: Portugal Golden Visa (7 days/year), UAE Golden Visa (no minimum). Moderate requirements: Canada PR (730 days over 5 years), Australia (varies by state). Significant presence needed: UK Innovator (180 days/year to maintain path to settlement), USA EB-5 (permanent residence, so substantial presence expected). We help you choose programs that match your lifestyle and business needs.

Can I include my family in the application? ‚ñº

Yes! All major business visa programs allow family inclusion: Spouse/partner gets same visa rights and work authorization. Dependent children included (age limits vary: typically under 18-23). Some programs (UAE, Portugal) allow parents as well. Family members must meet health and character requirements. Additional fees apply per dependent. Benefits include access to education, healthcare, and eventual citizenship pathway for the whole family.

What if my business fails? Will I lose my visa? ‚ñº

Depends on visa type and stage. Initial visa period: Most programs have genuine effort/due diligence clauses. If you made legitimate attempts and market conditions changed, authorities are often understanding. You may need to pivot to a new venture or demonstrate progress. After PR/settlement: Once you have permanent residence or have met settlement criteria, business performance typically doesn't affect your immigration status. Investment visas: Capital must be maintained for the required period regardless of ROI. We recommend diversification and realistic business planning to minimize risks.

How do I prove the source of my investment funds? ‚ñº

All programs require proof of legal source of funds: Business income (audited accounts, tax returns, profit distributions), Sale of assets (property sale agreements, business sale contracts), Inheritance (will, probate documents, estate accounts), Gift from family (gift deed, donor's wealth proof, relationship evidence), Savings accumulated over time (bank statements, salary history). Documents must typically cover 2-5 years. We guide you through the specific requirements and help structure the documentation properly. Early preparation is crucial as this is the most scrutinized aspect.

Do I need an existing business to apply? ‚ñº

New business (startup visas): UK Innovator, Canada SUV - you can apply with just a viable business idea and plan. No existing business needed. Existing business expansion: UK Expansion Worker, Australia BIV - requires operating business with turnover and ownership history (usually 2-4 years). Investment visas: Portugal, UAE, USA EB-5 - no business experience required, just capital. We help both first-time entrepreneurs and established business owners find the right pathway.

What is the success rate for business visa applications? ‚ñº

Success rates vary by program and quality of application: High success (85-98%): Portugal Golden Visa, UAE Golden Visa, investment-based programs with clear criteria. Moderate success (60-80%): UK Innovator (depends on endorsement quality), Canada Start-Up Visa, Australia BIV. Requires strong preparation (40-60%): USA EB-5 (depends on project due diligence). Our clients achieve 98% success rate because we only proceed with applications that meet all requirements. We conduct thorough pre-assessment and only advance viable cases.

Can I work for another company while on a business visa? ‚ñº

Generally no, with some exceptions. Business visa holders must: Focus primarily on their sponsored business, Work in director/owner capacity (not as employee elsewhere). Some flexibility exists: Can be director of multiple companies you own, Can invest in other businesses passively, Some countries allow consultancy work related to your expertise. After PR: Full work rights - can work anywhere or start new businesses. Check specific program rules as violations can jeopardize your visa status.

What are the tax implications of business migration? ‚ñº

Tax obligations depend on residency status and local laws: Tax residency: Usually triggered by physical presence (183+ days). Business taxes: Corporate tax on local business income (rates: 0% UAE, 15-30% elsewhere). Personal income: Salary and dividends may be taxed. Worldwide income: Some countries tax global income once you're resident. Tax planning strategies: Holding companies, treaty benefits, timing of residency changes. We connect you with international tax advisors specializing in cross-border business structures. Plan before moving, not after!

How long does the entire process take from start to visa approval? ‚ñº

Timeline varies significantly by program: Fast track (2-4 months): UAE Golden Visa. Standard (6-12 months): UK Innovator (with endorsement), Portugal Golden Visa. Longer timeline (12-18 months): Canada Start-Up Visa, Australia BIV. Extended process (18-36 months): USA EB-5. Key factors affecting timeline: Business plan preparation (2-3 months), Endorsement/nomination process (3-6 months), Government processing (varies), Source of funds documentation (1-2 months). We recommend starting 18-24 months before intended move for optimal planning.
