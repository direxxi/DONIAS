What Is A Medical Second Opinion?

A second opinion is when another doctor (different from your regular doctor) gives their view about your health problem and how it should be treated. This additional perspective can provide you with treatment options your original doctors didn't know were available.

In some cases, the information you gain during a second opinion consultation can even show that your initial diagnosis needs to be reconsidered. When you're making important health decisions, it's always advisable to get medical opinions from more than one doctor.

When Should You Recommend Your Patient For A Second Opinion

Sometimes your patient's health problems are straightforward, but there are other times when diagnosis and treatment are less clear. Getting a second opinion may be helpful to your patient when:

Treatment hasn't helped

If you have provided treatment but his/her symptoms continue

Unclear diagnosis

Your diagnosis isn't clear after initial examination

Complex medical history

Patient has a lot of medical conditions

High-risk treatment

The treatment you are recommending is experimental, controversial, or risky

Life-threatening condition

Your patient has a life-threatening condition

Not a specialist

You are not a specialist in your patient's condition

Limited treatment options

You are convinced you can't help him or her

Multiple treatment paths

Numerous possible treatment options are available

Patient has doubts

Your patient is expressing doubt about your diagnosis and treatment recommendations
