Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about family visas

How do we prove our relationship is genuine? ‚ñº

Immigration authorities look for consistent evidence spanning your entire relationship. Provide: Photos together (dated, showing progression of relationship), Communication records (WhatsApp, calls, emails - samples showing regular contact), Joint activities (trips, events, family gatherings), Financial ties (joint accounts, remittances, shared expenses), Third-party evidence (letters from friends/family who know you as a couple), Statutory declarations from both partners. Quality matters more than quantity - we help you create a compelling narrative with the right evidence mix.

What are the income requirements for sponsorship? ‚ñº

Requirements vary by country: UK: ¬£29,000 minimum (spouse only), additional amounts for children; USA: 125% of poverty guideline (varies by family size, ~$22k for 2 people); Canada: No minimum for spouse/partner sponsorship (unless receiving social assistance), LICO requirements for parent/grandparent; Australia: No set minimum, but must show financial capacity; UAE: AED 4,000-10,000 depending on emirate. Income can come from employment, self-employment, savings (some countries), or investments. We help calculate your specific requirement and identify all eligible income sources.

How long does the family visa process take? ‚ñº

Processing times vary by country and visa type: Fast (2-4 months): UAE family visa, NZ partnership; Standard (6-12 months): Canada spouse (inland), UK spouse visa; Long (12-24 months): USA spouse visa, Canada spouse (outland), UK appeals; Very Long (24-36 months): Australia partner visa, Canada parent sponsorship. Preparation phase before submission: 2-3 months typically. Factors affecting speed: completeness of application, current processing backlogs, whether interview required, any requests for additional info. We track processing times weekly and set realistic expectations.

Can we apply while visiting on a tourist visa? ‚ñº

Depends on the country: Yes (allowed): Canada inland sponsorship - can apply while visiting, get work permit while waiting; USA adjustment of status if entered legally; Australia onshore partner visa (if eligible visa). No (not allowed): UK - must apply from outside UK (with few exceptions); Most countries don't permit tourist visa to family visa switching. Risks: Entry may be denied if immigration suspects intent to stay; Better to apply from home country for most routes; Overstaying can lead to bans. We advise on the safest, legal pathways for your specific situation.

What happens if we get refused? ‚ñº

Refusals are not the end! Options include: Administrative Review: Challenge factual errors or procedural mistakes (time-limited); Appeal: Full hearing where you can submit new evidence (available in UK, some countries); Reapplication: Address refusal reasons and reapply with stronger case; Judicial Review: Challenge unlawful decisions (complex, expensive). Common refusal reasons we help overcome: Insufficient relationship evidence, income/financial requirements not met, English language issues, sham marriage concerns, incomplete documentation. Our Concierge package includes appeal support. For previous refusals, we offer specialized reapplication services with 87% success rate on second attempt.

Do children get automatic citizenship? ‚ñº

Citizenship rules vary: Born in destination country: USA & Canada - citizenship by birth; UK, Australia - only if parent is citizen/settled; Dependent children on family visa: Must apply for citizenship separately after residence period; Usually eligible when sponsoring parent naturalizes; May need to meet residence requirements. Timelines: UK - 5 years residence, Australia - 4 years, Canada - 3 years (with parent). Children under 18 often have simplified processes. We guide you through both immigration and eventual citizenship pathways for the whole family.

What if we're not legally married yet? ‚ñº

Several options available: Fianc√©(e) visa: For couples planning to marry (UK K-1 USA, Prospective Marriage Australia). Must marry within 3-6 months, then switch to spouse visa; Unmarried partner visa: Must prove cohabitation for 2+ years (UK, Australia, NZ). Same requirements as marriage except marriage certificate; Common-law partnership: Canada recognizes 12+ months cohabitation as equivalent to marriage; Civil partnership: Available in some countries, equivalent to marriage. If in a relationship but can't prove cohabitation: Consider getting married first (can be small ceremony), then apply for spouse visa which is often easier to prove. We help determine which pathway suits your situation best.

Can my spouse work immediately upon arrival? ‚ñº

Work rights vary by visa type: Full work rights from arrival: UK spouse visa - unrestricted employment; Canada PR through sponsorship - full work rights; Australia partner visa (820/309) - work rights included; USA green card (IR/CR) - can work immediately. Restricted: Some dependent visas on work/student sponsors may have conditions; Usually need to apply for separate work permit. No work rights: Tourist visas, visitor visas; Some fianc√© visas until marriage completed. Check specific visa conditions as unauthorized work can lead to deportation. We clarify work rights for your exact visa route.

What documents will we need? ‚ñº

Core documents for most family visas: Relationship proof: Marriage certificate (original + certified translation), Photos spanning relationship, Communication evidence, Cohabitation proof if unmarried; Sponsor documents: Citizenship/residence proof, Passport, Income evidence (payslips, tax returns, employment letter), Accommodation proof (tenancy/ownership); Applicant documents: Passport, Birth certificate, Police clearance certificates, Medical examination (approved doctors), English language test results (if required); Supporting documents: Relationship statements from both partners, Letters from family/friends, Evidence of visits/trips together. Documents in other languages need certified translations. We provide comprehensive checklists tailored to your visa type and country.

Medical exam: $100-$300. Police clearances: $50-$150 per country. Translations & notarization: $200-$500. Travel for biometrics/interview: Varies.
